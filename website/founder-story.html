JOHN DOE 1'S STORY

Rock Bottom at 19

"I grew up in an alcoholic family. By my teenage years, I was deep into drugs and alcohol—using anything I could get my hands on to escape the pain. At 19, I hit rock bottom. My family intervened, and I spent three months at a rehabilitation center."

That was August 1980. I haven't had a drink or drug since.

What Made the Difference

I got help because I had access: a family that could intervene, a treatment center within driving distance, and the financial means to afford care. But I've watched thousands—especially veterans—who didn't have those advantages.

The Key Observation

Over decades, I noticed: medical community, psychological therapists, and self-help groups often operated in silos. But people who thrived used everything. Integration works. Silos fail.

❤

Why This Is Personal

"I've lost count of people who died from addiction—not because treatment doesn't work, but because they couldn't access it. The veteran 50 miles from a VA facility. The soldier who feared career consequences. The mother who couldn't afford childcare."

Every one of those deaths was preventable. That's why I do this.

JOHN DOE 2'S STORY

From Chile to Crisis to Clarity

"I grew up in Chile, studied advertising at UNIAC before migrating to the United States in 1987. I continued my education at University of Maryland, pursuing graphic design and business administration. But I was also running from addiction."

By the late 1980s and early 1990s, my drinking and drug use had spiraled out of control.

January 1993: The Turning Point

I found my way to a self-help recovery program. I didn't have insurance. I didn't have money for treatment. What I had was a group of people in church basements and community centers—people who understood.

Peer support saved my life. It cost me nothing but honesty and willingness.

Recovery Unlocked Everything

In 1998, I founded Pixelme Studio. Over 25+ years, I built a premier photography and immersive media company. Clients included GE, Pfizer, BAE Systems, DOD contractors. I pioneered 60+ VR deployments.

None of that would have been possible if I was still drinking.

Technology Meets Mission (COVID Revelation)

"By 2020, I'd built a successful VR business. Then COVID hit, and something remarkable happened: recovery meetings went online overnight. I watched people in rural areas—who'd never attended meetings because the nearest one was 50 miles away—suddenly showing up online every day."

That's when I realized: Technology can eliminate the barriers keeping people from help.

❤

Why I Do This

"I've sponsored dozens over the years. Some made it. Some didn't. The ones who didn't? Often it wasn't because they didn't want help—it was because help wasn't accessible."

If this platform saves even one life—it will have been worth it. But I believe we can save thousands.
